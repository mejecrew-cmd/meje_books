# Worldview Designer Translation Glossary

v1.1 (2026-07-21). **Author-confirmed and binding for all four target languages.** Book title = **The Worldview Designer** (author decision). Remaining former-[AUTHOR] items resolved to the recommended forms below. Structure and conventions inherit from the Fifth Cult Community glossary; overlapping confirmed terms are carried over verbatim.

**Author-confirmed resolutions:**
- 세계관설계자 (book title) → **The Worldview Designer** (ja 世界観設計者 / zh-Hant 世界觀設計者 / zh-Hans 世界观设计者). Apply identically across all editions and catalog/metadata.
- 유니버스 디자이너 (job title) → EN Universe Designer / ja ユニバース・デザイナー / **zh-Hant 世界觀設計師 / zh-Hans 世界观设计师** (clearer over literal 宇宙~).
- 허락 / 허락받은 세계관 → **the reader's permission / the permitted worldview** (EN); one word consistently.
- 자가동력 (의 세계) → **self-propulsion / the self-propelling world** (EN); one rendering consistently.
- 스크립트 (ch.15 coinage) → keep **"script"** with a first-use gloss "(latent narrative path)"; disambiguate from programming/screen script.

The book「세계관설계자」(15 chapters/편, author 김동은WhtDrgon.) is organized in four parts: 총론 (General Theory, ch. 1–4), 유형론 (Typology, ch. 5–9), 실무론/방법론 (Practice/Methodology, ch. 10–13), and the media-and-self-propulsion part (ch. 14–15). It teaches 세계관 설계 (worldview design) as a professional craft.

## Style rules

- **Book-title recommendation [AUTHOR]:** recommend **"The Worldview Designer."** Reasoning: (1) it preserves 세계관=worldview, the term chapter 1 establishes and the whole book hinges on; (2) "Designer" matches 설계자 and the book's core claim that this work is *designed* (설계), not *written* (쓰기); (3) it harmonizes with the in-book coined job title "Universe Designer" without adopting the philosophical baggage of a bare "Worldview." Alternative "The Worldbuilder" is punchier and matches the established English craft category but discards the designer/architect nuance and blurs the worldview-vs-worldbuilding distinction the book insists on; offer it only if marketing wants the familiar category word. Whatever is chosen must be applied identically across all four editions and in catalog/metadata.
- Register: calm, precise literary-nonfiction prose in every language. This is a craft manual, so keep the instructional clarity; do not add rhetorical intensifiers, and do not upgrade the author's observations into stronger claims.
- English: chapter headings use a colon between number-title and subtitle when a subtitle exists (e.g., "## 5. World Types I: Time"); no em-dash or hyphen as the title separator. Inside EN body prose, native English punctuation (including em-dashes) is allowed.
- Japanese: use です・ます register, 「」 for quotation, and ── (2em dash) where a dash is needed. Genre terms of Japanese origin (魔法少女, 異世界, 巨大ロボット, 侍もの, 能力バトル) take their native forms, not transliterations of the Korean.
- Simplified Chinese: 书面语 register, ASCII/curly straight quotation marks, —— for the dash. Use mainland-standard technical vocabulary (数据, 分辨率, 网络).
- Traditional Chinese: Taiwan-leaning register, 「」 for quotation, —— for the dash. Use Taiwan-standard technical vocabulary (資料, 解析度, 網路). Traditional and Simplified are separate translations, not script conversions.
- Preserve the heading hierarchy exactly: the chapter line (# N편 → # Chapter N. …), the ten H2 section headings per chapter, and the "N편의 관통 질문은 이것이다:" framing line (render as "The guiding question of this chapter is:" / 「本章の貫く問いはこれである：」 / 「本章貫穿的提問是：」/「本章贯穿的提问是：」). Render the recurring "시사점은…" sentences consistently ("The design implication is…" / 「設計上の示唆は…」 / 「設計上的啟示是…」/「设计上的启示是…」).
- Preserve all numbers, years, counts, percentages, and named lists (e.g., the eight domains, the four axes, the three-act work cycle) exactly; keep list membership and order.
- Proper nouns and cited works use established target-language forms: Weltanschauung, Wilhelm Dilthey, Coleridge, Aristotle's *Poetics*, Henry Jenkins, Thomas More, García Márquez, Tolkien / Middle-earth, Dungeons & Dragons / Forgotten Realms, *The Lord of the Rings*, *Harry Potter*, *Star Wars*, *Animal Farm*, *Gulliver's Travels*, Aesop, the Brothers Grimm, Sherlock Holmes / Conan Doyle, Kurosawa's *Seven Samurai* → *The Magnificent Seven*, Knox's Decalogue, Van Dine's Twenty Rules, Marvel, DC. Do not invent new renderings.
- Keep coined-term resonances that pay off later: 코드-like software resonance in 스크립트/데이터 파이프라인/전처리/데이터시트/버전 관리 (keep the data-engineering vocabulary literal); 본말전도/설정놀음 kept non-pejorative; 허락/다이브/자가동력 rendered consistently once the [AUTHOR] choices are fixed.
- © or footer lines: none appear in the current source; if any are added later, keep them byte-identical.
- Each chapter translation file carries `title` frontmatter (localized) and lives at `worldview-designer/translations/<lang>/<original-chapter-slug>.md`, per TRANSLATIONS.md; keep the Korean chapter slug for a stable one-to-one URL.
