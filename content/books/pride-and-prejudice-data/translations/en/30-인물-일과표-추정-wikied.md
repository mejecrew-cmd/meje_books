# *Pride and Prejudice* — Character Daily Schedules (Inferred/Created)

> ⚠️ **This document is inferential and creative.** It reconstructs daily schedules by combining the standard routines of Regency gentry and the middle class with each character's personality and patterns of behavior in the novel. These schedules may support depictions of everyday life in adaptations.

---

## A Standard Day for the Regency Gentry

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 7–8 a.m. | Rise. A servant brings warm water to the bedroom. Put on morning dress. |
| 8–9 a.m. | Walking or private time: letters and reading |
| 9–10 a.m. | Breakfast with the whole family |
| 10 a.m.–noon | Walks, morning calls, household work, reading, music practice |
| Noon–3 p.m. | Going out, shopping, walking, receiving visitors |
| 3:30–4 p.m. | Dressing for dinner |
| 4–5 p.m. | Dinner, the principal meal of the day |
| 5–7 p.m. | Rest after dinner: men take port; women retire to the drawing room |
| 7–9 p.m. | Tea, cards, music, reading, conversation |
| 9–10 p.m. | Supper, a light meal usually served only after a ball |
| 10–11 p.m. | Bed |

## 1. Elizabeth Bennet — Inferred Schedule

| Time | Activity | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| 6:30–7 a.m. | **Rises early** | Active temperament and frequent walks |
| 7–8 a.m. | **Walks alone**, weather permitting | Daily walks at Hunsford; a way to avoid Wickham |
| 8–9 a.m. | Morning dress and hair, helping Jane and receiving her help | Presumed absence of a lady's maid |
| 9 a.m. | Family breakfast | Standard routine |
| 10 a.m.–noon | **Reads or talks with her sisters** | “great reader, has no pleasure in anything else,” Miss Bingley's slander; in truth, Elizabeth reads with wit |
| | Or walks the mile to Meryton | With her sisters |
| Noon–3 p.m. | Receives visitors or goes out | |
| 3:30 p.m. | Dressing | |
| 4 p.m. | Family dinner | |
| After 7 p.m. | **Tea; declines cards; reads** | Ch. 8: refuses loo and prefers a book |
| 10 p.m. | Bed | |

### Schedules During Special Periods
- **At Netherfield (Chs. 7–12):** spends most of her time nursing Jane and joins the drawing room only in the evening
- **At Hunsford (Chs. 28–37):** uses the walking paths daily to avoid Collins
- **After visiting Pemberley (Chs. 43–46):** more time spent in reflection on Darcy—Ch. 44, “lay awake two whole hours”

## 2. Mr. Darcy — Inferred Schedule

| Time | Activity | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| 7 a.m. | Rises with his valet's assistance | Standard routine |
| 7–8 a.m. | Riding or private exercise | “fine, tall person”—habitual exercise |
| 8–9 a.m. | Shaving and dressing | Attended by his valet |
| 9 a.m. | Breakfast | Standard routine |
| 10 a.m.–noon | **Letters and estate business** | Ch. 10, “writing a letter”; Ch. 43, “business with his steward” |
| Noon–3 p.m. | Riding, reading, touring the estate | Reputation as “the best landlord” |
| 3–4 p.m. | Dressing | |
| 4 p.m. | Dinner | |
| After 7 p.m. | Reading; habitually buys books | Ch. 8: “always buying books” |
| 11 p.m. | Bed | |

### Special Periods
- **At Netherfield (Chs. 7–12):** stays with Bingley, spends more time observing Elizabeth, and is deliberately silent in the evening
- **At Rosings (Chs. 28–37):** visits Hunsford Parsonage daily, including ten-minute silences
- **Secret search in London (around EVT-039):** bribes Mrs. Younge and negotiates with Wickham; ordinary routine collapses entirely
- **After returning to Pemberley (Ch. 43 onward):** socializes with the Gardiners and brings Georgiana

## 3. Mr. Bennet — Inferred Schedule

| Time | Activity | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| 7–8 a.m. | Rises without haste | His “indolent” nature |
| 8–9 a.m. | **Goes straight to the library** | Avoids conversation with his wife |
| 9 a.m. | Breakfast | With the family |
| After 9 a.m. | **Returns to the library and remains secluded all day** | “fond of the country and of books... his principal enjoyments” |
| Noon–3 p.m. | Walks and inspects the farm | |
| 4 p.m. | Attends family dinner | |
| After 5 p.m. | **Retreats to the library again** | Ch. 20: demands Collins's “free use of my room” |
| After 10 p.m. | Reads late | Ch. 3: “He had rather hoped... He had a good deal of curiosity” |

### Daily Pattern
- **Minimizes conversation with his wife**, speaking chiefly at meals
- **Enjoys conversation with Elizabeth**, especially in the library
- **Acts only in a crisis**—normally an observer, but searches London immediately after EVT-038

### Sundays as a Clergyman

- Visits parishioners or takes tea at Rosings in the afternoon
