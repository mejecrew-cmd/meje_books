# *Pride and Prejudice* — Chronology

> An in-story chronology reconstructed by working backward from every temporal clue in the original text.
> The **reference year 1811** is inferred from the novel's publication in 1813 and the internal clue that November 18 falls on a Monday.
>
> **Chronology types:**
> - **[Confirmed]** — a date or weekday is stated in the original
> - **[Calculated]** — can be calculated mechanically from a confirmed date
> - **[Range]** — textual clues narrow the range, but the exact date is unknown
> - **[Estimated]** — approximate point calculated from age, duration, or similar clues; margin of error ±2–5 years
> - **[Relative]** — only the position relative to another event is known, such as “after” or “immediately before”

---

## Basis for the Reference Year

| Clue | Original text | Calculation |
|---|---|---|
| Collins's arrival date | “Monday, November 18th” (letter in Chapter 13) | Years between 1800 and 1820 in which November 18 was a Monday: **1811** and 1822 |
| Novel published | January 1813 | 1811 is the most natural “present” |
| Gardiner's letter | “Monday, August 2” (Chapter 49) | August 2, 1812 was a **Sunday** → not a perfect match; presumed calendar error by Austen |
| Conclusion | **Story begins: autumn 1811 / ending: autumn–winter 1812** ||

> **Caution:** Austen did not maintain a perfectly precise calendar. “Monday, August 2” fell on a Sunday in 1812, so it contains a weekday error. In translation or adaptation, prioritize **season and relative order** and use weekdays only as references.
