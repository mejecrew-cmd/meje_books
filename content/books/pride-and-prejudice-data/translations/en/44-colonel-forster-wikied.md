# Colonel Forster

## Basic Information
- **Original name:** Colonel Forster
- **Korean rendering:** Colonel Forster
- **Position:** commander of the militia regiment stationed at Meryton, then Brighton
- **Age:** middle-aged, recently married to his young wife Harriet
- **First appearance:** chunk_04, around Chap. 16 in the introduction of the militia

## Character Introduction
Commander of the militia regiment stationed at Meryton in Hertfordshire and superior officer to Wickham, Denny, and the novel's other officers. Though portrayed as respectable and responsible, his supervision fails twice, revealing the limits of the institution. First, he fails to control Wickham's gambling debts and dissipation within the regiment, informing the Bennets only after the flight. Second, he effectively neglects Lydia, whom his own wife should have supervised.

Colonel Forster's narrative function is that of the crisis “messenger.” After Lydia's elopement, he hurries from Brighton to Longbourn to report the situation and provides negative information about Wickham. He bears no malice himself, but represents a social structure in which wrongdoing grows in the absence of systematic supervision.

## Development by Event
- **EVT-022, indirect:** the militia remains in Meryton through winter, and its officers continue their social life. Colonel Forster has recently married Harriet and settles in Meryton with her.
- **EVT-032:** the militia decides to move to Brighton. He permits or approves Mrs. Forster's invitation for Lydia to accompany her. Elizabeth warns Mr. Bennet that even Colonel Forster's supervision cannot control Lydia's recklessness, but fails to stop the journey.
- **EVT-038:** immediately after Lydia and Wickham's flight is discovered, Colonel Forster pursues them as far as Clapham but loses the trail. He rushes to Longbourn, reports the situation to Mr. Bennet, and testifies that Wickham had a poor reputation in the regiment because of gambling debts and immoral conduct. This is the first official confirmation of Wickham's true nature by an outsider.

## Core Relationships
- **Wickham:** subordinate officer; Forster learns the extent of his dissipation only after the flight, a failure of command supervision
- **Mrs. Forster, Harriet:** young wife; he does not prevent her from inviting Lydia
- **The Bennets:** messenger of Lydia's elopement and a well-intentioned witness
