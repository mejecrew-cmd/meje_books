# *Pride and Prejudice* — Character Psychology Curves (Analysis/Creative Support)

> Tracks the **event-by-event changes in the psychological states** of major characters. Use it to guide characterization in adaptations and tone in translation.
>
> **Scale:** ▲ (rise) / ▼ (fall) / ━ (plateau) / ✦ (turning point)

---

## 1. Elizabeth Bennet — Core Emotional Curves

### A. Feelings Toward Darcy (-10 to +10)

```
[-10: extreme hostility]  [0: neutral]  [+10: deep love]

EVT-003 Meryton assembly           -3   “tolerable” insult (mild dislike)
EVT-005 Refusal at Lucas Lodge     -4   Keeps her distance
EVT-009 Reel confrontation         -5   Hostility intensifies
EVT-014 After Wickham’s testimony  -7  ✦ Prejudice takes root
EVT-016 Dance at the ball          -8   Peak confrontation
EVT-027 Fitzwilliam’s disclosure   -9   Height of anger
EVT-028 Rejects first proposal    -10  ✦ Lowest point (“last man in the world”)
EVT-029 First reading of letter   -10   Still refuses to accept it
EVT-029 Rereads letter             -5  ✦ Begins to doubt Wickham
EVT-030 Self-recognition           -2  ✦ “blind, partial, prejudiced”
EVT-031 Shares with Jane           -1   Admission + shame
EVT-033 Final encounter with Wickham +1  Positive view of Darcy (“improves”)
EVT-035 Visits Pemberley           +3  ✦ Shocked by Mrs. Reynolds
EVT-036 Meets Darcy again          +4   Astonished by his change
EVT-037 Darcy’s consideration      +5   Respect + gratitude
EVT-038 Elopement crisis + breaks down before Darcy  +6  Weakness of resolve
EVT-039 Recognizes her love        +7  ✦ “could have loved him”
EVT-041 Full recognition           +8  ✦ “exactly the man”
EVT-042 Secret intervention revealed +9 ✦ Profound gratitude
EVT-046 Accepts second proposal   +10  ✦ Highest point
EVT-048 Marriage                  +10   Stable love
```

### B. Self-Knowledge Curve (Conceit → Recognition)

```
Ch.1-30   ▲▲▲   Stage of “priding herself on her discernment”
Ch.30-36  ▼▼▼   ✦ Recognition of prejudice begins
Ch.36     ━     “Till this moment, I never knew myself”
Ch.37-50  ━━    Period of maturation
Ch.50+    ▲▲    Rebuilt self-knowledge (confidence without pride)
```

### C. Shame About Her Family

```
Ch.1     +2   Moderate irritation
Ch.9     +5   Embarrassment of the Netherfield visit
Ch.18    +9  ✦ Total humiliation at the ball
Ch.34   +10  ✦ Darcy criticizes her family during the proposal (she agrees inwardly)
Ch.36    +9   Acknowledges the justice of it after Darcy’s letter
Ch.41    +8   Lydia goes to Brighton
Ch.46   +10  ✦ Elopement crisis—the peak
Ch.61    +5   Partly relieved through marriage (but a lifelong burden)
```

## 13. Synchronization of Emotional Change (By Couple)

### Comparing the Darcy–Elizabeth Curves

```
Point       Darcy   Elizabeth
Ch.3          0       -3      (Darcy indifferent, Elizabeth mildly dislikes him)
Ch.7         +4       -3      (gap begins)
Ch.18        +6       -8      (first peak of divergence)
Ch.28       +10      -10      (second peak—proposal/rejection)
Ch.36       +10       -2      (Darcy unchanged, Elizabeth recognizes herself)
Ch.43       +10       +3      (convergence begins)
Ch.46       +10       +6      (convergence accelerates)
Ch.58       +10      +10      (complete convergence)
```

> **Translation/adaptation note:** Their emotional curves are **completely asymmetrical**—Darcy loves her almost continuously (restraint → expression), whereas Elizabeth undergoes a major transformation. On screen, Darcy’s gaze and expression must show this consistency.

## 14. Key Turning Points (All Characters Combined)

### Ch.34 First Proposal — Simultaneous Shock
- Darcy: starting point of self-awareness
- Elizabeth: peak of prejudice (recognition follows)
- The greatest shock of both their lives

### Ch.36 Recognition — Elizabeth Alone
- Darcy does not know
- Elizabeth changes alone

### Ch.43 Pemberley — First Synchronization
- Their curves move in the same direction for the first time

### Ch.46 Elopement — Crisis and Recognition of Love
- Elizabeth: acknowledges love (but despairs)
- Darcy: resolves to act (proof of love)

### Ch.58 Second Proposal — Complete Convergence
- The two curves meet

## 15. Uses in Translation and Adaptation

### Translation
- Even the same form of address for the same character should change in tone according to the point in the story
- Choose words with special care in scenes at a curve’s peak or trough

### Screen Adaptation
- Direct changes in facial expression and gaze to match the curves
- Let the music change with the curves as well

### Stage Adaptation
- Visualize emotional curves through blocking + lighting

### Derivative Works
- Stories can fill the blank spaces in the curves—the periods the novel does not explain
