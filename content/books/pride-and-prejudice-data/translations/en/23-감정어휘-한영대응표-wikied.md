# *Pride and Prejudice* — Emotion Vocabulary Correspondence Guide

> English words for emotions used in the novel, their corresponding meanings, and intensity ratings.
> Even the same emotion calls for a different word according to its intensity and coloring—the most difficult territory in translation.

---

## 1. Classification System for Emotion Vocabulary

### Five Degrees of Intensity
- ⓟ Faint
- ① Mild
- ② Moderate
- ③ Strong
- ④ Intense
- ⑤ Extreme

### Coloring (The Texture of the Emotion)
- 🌸 Warm
- ❄️ Cold
- 🔥 Passionate
- 🌧️ Melancholy
- ⚡ Furious
- 🌫️ Ambiguous

## 2. Liking and Love (By Intensity)

| English | Meaning/Nuance | Intensity | Coloring | Example from the Novel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **acquaintance** | familiarity; knowing someone | ⓟ | 🌫️ | “her acquaintance”—merely knowing someone |
| **liking** | goodwill, liking | ① | 🌸 | “I give you leave to like him” |
| **partiality** | preference, liking with a bias | ① | 🌸 | “his partiality for Miss Bennet” |
| **inclination** | attraction, a leaning of the heart | ① | 🌫️ | “a slight, thin sort of inclination” |
| **regard** | respect, warm feeling | ② | 🌸 | “his sincere, if not equal, regard” |
| **esteem** | respect, high valuation | ② | 🌸 | “she respected, she esteemed” |
| **affection** | fondness, tenderness | ② | 🌸 | “tender affection” |
| **fondness** | tenderness, affection | ② | 🌸 | “fond of his sister’s society” |
| **attachment** | deep feeling, emotional bond | ③ | 🌸 | “stronger attachment than I had ever known” |
| **admiration** | admiration, wonder | ③ | 🔥 | “looked at with great admiration” |
| **love** | love | ③–④ | 🔥 | “how ardently I admire and love you” |
| **passion** | passion, intense feeling | ④ | 🔥 | “the utmost force of passion” |
| **ardour** | fervor | ④ | 🔥 | “spoken... with such ardour” (indirect) |
| **adoration** | adoration, worship | ⑤ | 🔥 | “the adoration of Mrs. Forster” |

### Rendering Guide

```
[Mild Liking]
acquaintance: “familiarity” / “knowing someone”
liking: “liking” / “goodwill”
partiality: “preference—with a bias” / “favoritism”
inclination: “attraction” / “a leaning of the heart”

[Moderate Liking]
regard: “warm feeling” / “consideration” / “respect”
esteem: “respect” / “admiration”
affection: “affection” / “tenderness”
fondness: “tender feeling”
attachment: “deep attachment” / “emotional bond”

[Strong Love]
admiration: “admiration” / “wonder”
love: “love” / “romantic devotion”
passion: “passion” / “intense feeling”
ardour: “fervent feeling”
adoration: “adoration” / “devotion”
```

### Evolution by Stage in the Novel (Darcy → Elizabeth)

```
Ch.6   admiration—her “fine eyes”
Ch.10  bewitched—an almost magical attraction
Ch.34  ardent love—“how ardently I admire and love you”
Ch.58  unchanged affections—a quiet depth
```

### Rendering Guide

```
[Mild]

[Strong]

anger: “anger” / “wrath”

abhorrence: “abhorrence” / “aversion”

[Intense]
detestation: “detestation” / “hatred”
fury: “fury” / “anger surging up”

```

```

## 11. Frequency of Emotion Vocabulary by Character

### Elizabeth (Vivacity + Wit)

```
[Frequently Used Words]
- delight, amusement, vexation
- mortification, in matters of pride
- “lively, playful disposition”

[Translation Tone]
- Animated + lightly satirical
- “interesting,” “amusing,” “vexing”
```

### Darcy (Restraint + Intensity)

```
[Frequently Used Words]
- regard, esteem, ardour
- shame, mortification
- “in vain have I struggled”

[Translation Tone]
- Restrained + emotionally deep
- “regard,” “admiration,” “shame”
```

### Mrs. Bennet (Violent Surface Emotion)

```
[Frequently Used Words]
- joy, rapture, transport
- vexation, distress
- nerves

[Translation Tone]
- Passionate but shallow
- “rapture,” “vexation,” “distress”
```

### Mr. Bennet (Cynical Detachment)

```
[Frequently Used Words]
- amusement, especially at the folly of others
- Almost no direct expression of emotion

[Translation Tone]
- Wit held at a distance
- “I find it amusing” / “How interesting”
```

### Jane (Restrained Tenderness)

```
[Frequently Used Words]
- regard, esteem, attachment
- Emotion kept under restraint

[Translation Tone]
- Tender but formal
- “regard,” “to hold someone in one’s heart”
```

### Lydia (No Restraint)

```
[Frequently Used Words]
- joy, rapture, ecstasy
- All at the surface

[Translation Tone]
- Excitable without lapsing into vulgarity
- “Rapture!” / “Indeed!”
```

### Lady Catherine (Anger + Domination)

```
[Frequently Used Words]
- displeasure, indignation
- arrogance, condescension

[Translation Tone]
- Authoritative anger
- “displeasure,” “wrath”
```

### Collins (Excessive Formality)

```
[Frequently Used Words]
- gratification, satisfaction
- humility, civility—only outwardly

[Translation Tone]
- Excessively formal diction
- “gratification,” “deeply obliged”
```

### Ch.34: The Proposal

```
[Darcy]
1. agitation—entering
2. ardent admiration—the proposal
3. astonishment—being refused
4. anger—“pale with anger”
5. mortification—“ashamed of what my own have been”

## 13. Preserving Subtle Distinctions in Translation

### “regard” vs. “esteem” vs. “affection” vs. “love”

```
[Darcy in the Novel]
Ch.34: “ardently admire and love”—love, strong
Ch.46: “real, though unavailing concern”—deep concern during a family crisis
Ch.58: “affections and wishes are unchanged”—unchanged affection

[Elizabeth in the Novel]
Ch.44: “She respected, she esteemed, she was grateful to him”
       → respect, esteem, gratitude—the stage immediately before love
Ch.46: “could have loved him”
       → the first use of “love”
Ch.58: accepts his love
```

### Differences in Rendering

```
Source: “regard”     → “warm feeling” / “consideration” (neutral)
Source: “esteem”     → “respect” / “admiration”
Source: “affection”  → “affection” / “tenderness”
Source: “love”       → “love” / “romantic devotion”
Source: “passion”    → “passion”
Source: “ardour”     → “fervent feeling”
```

Rendering all of them simply as “love” destroys the subtle progression.

## 15. Golden Rules of Translation

### 1. Preserve the Progression
- The progression “regard → esteem → affection → love” must remain a progression in translation.

### 2. Distinguish Each Character’s Diction
- Darcy’s “ardent” ≠ Collins’s “ardent.”
- Even the same word must suit the character’s level of refinement.

### 3. Keep Intensity Consistent
- Do not reduce “rapture” to “pleasure”—use “rapture.”
- Do not reduce “fury” to “being upset”—use “fury.”

### 4. Preserve Emotional Coloring
- Keep warm words warm.
- Keep cold words cold.
- “esteem” = warm respect; “respect” = neutral regard—render them differently.

### 5. Maintain Period Refinement
- “love” may be rendered as “devotion” or “romantic affection.”
- “pleased” may be rendered as “gratified” or “well contented.”
