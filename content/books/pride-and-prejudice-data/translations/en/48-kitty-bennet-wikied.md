# Kitty Bennet / Catherine Bennet

## Basic Information
- **Original names:** Kitty Bennet, Catherine Bennet, Miss Catherine
- **Korean rendering:** Kitty Bennet / Catherine Bennet
- **Age:** 17; the fourth daughter
- **Family:** fourth daughter of the Bennet family
- **Residence:** Longbourn
- **First appearance:** chunk_04 (around Chap. 6)

## Character Introduction
The fourth Bennet daughter, a frivolous girl interested only in officers and balls under Lydia’s influence. Though older than Lydia, she is weaker in character, follows Lydia everywhere, and shows almost no initiative of her own. She cries because she cannot accompany Lydia to Brighton and grows jealous when Lydia marries first. By the conclusion, however, she has escaped Lydia’s harmful influence and is described as having “considerably improved” under Jane and Elizabeth’s guidance.

## Core Relationships
- **Lydia:** her closest sister and the source of her harmful influence; Kitty imitates everything Lydia does
- **Jane / Elizabeth:** by the conclusion, she spends long periods in her two elder sisters’ homes and benefits from their positive influence
- **Her mother:** her mother’s favorite daughter after Lydia

## Translation Notes
- “Kitty” is a diminutive of Catherine. She is called Kitty almost throughout the novel.
- Kitty’s speech is characterized by repeated complaints and exclamations. Her tone resembles Lydia’s, though it is less forceful.
