## 2. Types of Music in the Regency Era

### Drawing-Room Music

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Ballads** | Short lyrical songs for a female soloist |
| **Italian Songs** | Operatic arias favored by fashionable women such as Miss Bingley |
| **Scotch/Irish Airs** | Folk-like melodies with a light atmosphere; played by Mary |
| **English Folk Songs** | Traditional songs such as “Greensleeves” |
| **Glees** | Three- or four-part choral pieces |
| **Sonatas** | Classical music by Beethoven, Mozart, Haydn, and others |

### Ball Music

| Dance | Music |
|---|---|
| **Country Dance** | Lively duple or quadruple meter |
| **Cotillion** | Dance for four couples |
| **Quadrille** | Dance for four couples; fashionable from 1815 |
| **Reel** | Fast Scottish-style dance |
| **Boulanger** | Final country dance |
| **Minuet** | Formal and favored by older people; already old-fashioned |
| **Waltz** | Introduced to England in 1812; only just emerging at the novel’s date and not mentioned in it |

### Probable Composers
- **Mozart**, who died in 1791 and remained at the height of popularity
- **Haydn**, who died in 1809 and had visited England
- **Beethoven**, active and considered new music
- **Handel**, already a classic
- **Thomas Arne**, an English composer
- **Charles Dibdin**, known for English ballads

## 3. Musical Disposition by Character

### Elizabeth Bennet
- **Piano:** “by no means capital” (Ch.6)—a moderate level
- **Singing:** Accompanies herself; “pleasing”
- **Preferred Music:** Light ballads
- **Attitude:** “rather not sit down before those who must be in the habit of hearing the very best performers” (Ch.6)—modest
- **Translation Note:** She enjoys music without showing off; expression matters more than technique

### Mary Bennet
- **Piano:** **Practices much but lacks talent**—“neither genius nor taste” (Ch.6)
- **Singing:** A weak voice and awkward manner
- **Preferred Music:** Long concertos, sacred music, and moralizing lyrics
- **Scenes:** Ch.6 at Lucas Lodge, where her Scotch and Irish airs prompt the sisters to dance; Ch.18 at the Netherfield ball, where prolonged singing embarrasses the family
- **Translation Note:** A serious but comic combination

### Caroline Bingley
- **Piano:** Plays well and formally
- **Preferred Music:** Italian songs and Scotch airs, used to attract Darcy’s attention
- **Scene:** In Ch.10 she deliberately varies her selections to attract Darcy
- **Harp:** Possibly plays

### Georgiana Darcy
- **Piano:** **Excellent**—“her performance on the pianoforte is exquisite” (Caroline, Ch.8)
- “she practises very constantly” (Darcy, Ch.31)
- **Harp:** “delighted to hear of her improvement on the harp” (Ch.10)
- **Preferred Music:** Serious classical music
- **New Piano:** Ch.43, a gift from Darcy

### Anne de Bourgh
- **Unable to play**—“her health had not allowed her to apply” (Lady Catherine, Ch.31)
- Her mother boasts that she would have played well if healthy

### Lady Catherine
- **Does not play herself**—“If I had ever learnt, I should have been a great proficient” (Ch.31)
- **Pretentious:** Still offers musical criticism

### Lydia Bennet
- **Barely engages in music**—little interest
- Cares only for dancing at balls

### The Men
- **Darcy:** Does not play, as was standard for men; listens
- **Bingley:** Does not play; enjoys listening
- **Collins:** Merely praises it as an “amiable amusement”
- **Mr. Bennet:** Does not participate; remains in his library
- **Mr. Hurst:** Indifferent

## 4. Organization of Music at Balls

### The Meryton Assembly (Ch.3)
- Small hired band of four to six musicians
- Violin, cello, flute, and harpsichord or piano
- The master of ceremonies selects the pieces

### The Private Ball at Netherfield (Ch.18)
- Larger band of six to ten musicians
- Performs on a separate platform or in a gallery
- Short rests between pieces

### Order of the Ball
```
1. Country Dance (2–3 pieces)
2. Rest + drinks
3. Quadrille or Cotillion
4. Rest
5. Reel
6. Supper
7. Country Dance again
8. Final dance: Boulanger (everyone)
```

## 6. Everyday Soundscapes (Estimated)

### Longbourn, a Country House
**Morning:**
- Roosters crowing at five or six
- Birds singing
- Dishes and the hearth in the kitchen
- A maid knocking at a bedroom door and pouring warm water

**Forenoon:**
- Carriage wheels announcing visitors
- The sisters talking and Mrs. Bennet calling
- Intermittent piano practice
- Mr. Bennet turning pages in the library

**Afternoon:**
- Mrs. Bennet’s carriage going out
- Birds in the garden
- Farm laborers in the distance

**Evening:**
- Tableware at dinner
- Conversation in the drawing room
- Cards being shuffled
- The clock

**Night:**
- Logs crackling in the fireplace
- The clock striking every hour
- Dogs barking in the distance

### The Streets of Meryton
**Daytime:**
- Carriage wheels and horses’ hooves
- Merchants calling to customers
- Military footsteps on parade
- Bells from the church and at noon

### Pemberley
**Daytime:**
- The sound of the river, explicitly mentioned in Ch.43
- Gardeners’ tools
- Hounds in the distance
- Carriage wheels on the gravel drive

### The Ballroom
- The band
- Stamping feet in the dances
- A murmur of conversation
- Glasses clinking
- Fans fluttering

### During Wickham and Lydia’s Flight
- Carriage wheels around midnight
- Hooves in the darkness
- Changing horses at a posting inn

## 7. Sound by Key Scene (For Adaptation)

### Ch.3, Meryton Assembly
- **Music:** Lively country dance
- **Background:** Chatter, footsteps, fans
- **Darcy’s “tolerable” moment:** The music may briefly recede

### Ch.10, Netherfield Drawing Room
- **Miss Bingley’s piano:** Italian at first, then lively Scotch music
- **Darcy’s invitation to dance a “reel”:** Emphasize the music
- **Pen scratching** as Darcy writes his letter

### Ch.18, Netherfield Ball
- **Full sound:** Large band + crowd
- **Darcy and Elizabeth dance:** Their conversation within the music
- **Mary’s singing:** An awkward atmosphere; the musical tone may be adjusted so that her pitch is slightly off

### Ch.34, Proposal at Hunsford
- **Silence**—no music
- **Logs crackling in the fireplace**
- **Darcy’s footsteps** as he paces the room
- **Elizabeth’s breathing**

### Ch.43, Pemberley

- **A gardener’s rake** just before Darcy appears
