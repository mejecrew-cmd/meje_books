# Pride and Prejudice — Complete List of Groups

> A comprehensive catalogue of **every group, organization, social class, and profession** mentioned in the novel.

---

## A. Military Groups

| # | English | Designation | Nature | Role in the Novel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | ----shire Militia regiment | ----shire Militia Regiment | A semi-regular home-defense force stationed at Meryton for the winter | Wickham, Denny, Carter, and Forster belong to it. The object of Lydia and Kitty's infatuation. Disbanded after the Brighton encampment |
| 2 | The Regulars / Regular army | Regular Army | A combat force, including units deployed overseas | Wickham transfers to it after marrying. His regiment, commanded by a general, is stationed in the north; the move amounts to exile |
| 3 | General ----'s regiment | General ----'s Regiment | Wickham's destination after his transfer | Stationed in the north. Darcy purchases Wickham an ensigncy |
| 4 | "the officers" | Officers (general) | The militia officer corps | The focus of Lydia and Kitty's attention and regular guests at the Philipses' evening parties |

## B. Houses and Family Groups

| # | English | Designation | Members | Function in the Novel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | The Bennet family | The Bennet Family | Mr. and Mrs. Bennet and their five daughters | The novel's central family and victims of the entail |
| 6 | The Darcy family | The Darcy Family | Darcy and Georgiana, plus their deceased parents | The highest rank of the gentry and a core family in the novel |
| 7 | The Bingley party | The Bingley Party | Bingley, Miss Bingley, Mr. and Mrs. Hurst, and Darcy | The group leasing Netherfield; wealthy people of commercial origin |
| 8 | The Lucas family | The Lucas Family | Sir William and Lady Lucas, Charlotte, Maria, and the younger sons | The Bennets' neighbours at Longbourn; a knighted family |
| 9 | The de Bourgh family | The de Bourgh Family | Lady Catherine, Anne de Bourgh, and the late Sir Lewis | The landowning family of Rosings and Darcy's maternal relations |
| 10 | The Gardiner family | The Gardiner Family | Mr. and Mrs. Gardiner and four children—two daughters and two sons | A London merchant family; Mr. Gardiner is Mrs. Bennet's younger brother |
| 11 | The Philips family | The Philips Family | Mr. and Mrs. Philips | Residents of Meryton; Mrs. Philips is Mrs. Bennet's younger sister |
| 12 | The Fitzwilliam family | The Fitzwilliam Family (an earl's family) | Colonel Fitzwilliam's father, an earl, and his elder brother, the heir | Never appears directly, but explains the colonel's position as a “younger son” |

| # | English | Designation | Definition | Significance in the Novel |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| 20 | "people of fashion" / "first circles" | Fashionable Society | The core of the London social season | The world Darcy inhabits and that Sir William glimpses through his visits to court |

|---|---|---|---|---|
| 21 | The Church (of England) | Church of England | The established church | Collins's institution and the foundation of the patronage system for clerical livings |
