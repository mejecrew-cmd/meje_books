# Pride and Prejudice — Hidden Character Networks (Inferred/Created)

> ⚠️ **This document contains inference and invention.** Beyond relationships explicitly stated in the novel, it tracks **implied or omitted connections** based on historical context, character movements, and social plausibility. It may be used to give characters greater depth in adaptations.

---

## 1. Family Networks Not Explicitly Stated in the Novel

### Subtle Alliances among the Bennet Sisters
| Alliance | Strength | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Jane–Elizabeth** | ★★★★★ | Explicit; they share confidences |
| **Kitty–Lydia** | ★★★★ | Explicit; Kitty follows Lydia |
| **Mary alone** | ★ | No deep alliance with anyone; retreats into music and books |
| **Elizabeth–Mary (potential)** | ★ | Both are intellectual, but Mary is pedantic; the bond is not deep |
| **Jane–Mary (potential)** | ★★ | Jane's kindness could embrace Mary |
| **Jane–Lydia** | ★ | Almost nonexistent because of their different temperaments |

### Information Flow within the Bennet Family
```
[Shared Secrets]
Elizabeth ↔ Jane (most secrets)
Kitty → Lydia (information about officers)
Lydia → Mrs. Bennet (gossip)
Mary → no one (presumably keeps a private journal)

[Blocked Information]
- Darcy's first proposal: only Jane learns of it
- Wickham's true nature: only Jane learns of it, excluding the Georgiana episode
- Darcy's secret intervention: Elizabeth alone, then Jane later
- Mrs. Bennet is always the last to know
```

## 2. Servant–Master Networks (Inferred)

### Mrs. Hill ↔ Mrs. Bennet
- **Companions for more than twenty years**, estimating from the Bennets' marriage
- The person who most often endures Mrs. Bennet's nervous attacks
- Mrs. Bennet may often confide secrets to Hill
- Chapter 49: “Have you heard the good news?”—an intimate form of address

### Mrs. Reynolds ↔ the Darcy Family
- **Has known Darcy since he was four**—explicit in Chapter 43
- Effectively serves as Darcy's **second mother**
- May have replaced a maternal presence after Lady Anne's death
- Also influences Georgiana's upbringing
- Her **praise of Darcy to visitors** is sincere

### Sarah ↔ the Bennet Sisters
- **Attends to the sisters' daily needs**
- Chooses whom to assist first when preparing for a ball; Jane takes priority in Chapter 55
- A potential conduit for gossip

### Pemberley's Gardeners ↔ Darcy
- Darcy's direct involvement in the estate implies an established relationship with the gardeners
- While the Gardiners are at Pemberley, a gardener accompanies Mr. Gardiner fishing

## 3. The Meryton Social Network (Inferred)

### Flow of Gossip
```
Mrs. Long → Mrs. Bennet → the Bennet sisters → Meryton
         ↓
Mrs. Philips ← Mrs. Bennet (sisters)
         ↓
Guests at the Philipses' house (officers) → all of Meryton

[Other Channels]
Lady Lucas → Mrs. Bennet (competition + information)
Sir William Lucas → society at large (boasting of the “first circles”)
```

### Mrs. Long's Position
- **Meryton's gossip hub**—Chapter 1: the first person to deliver news of Bingley
- Lives with two unmarried nieces, who compete with the Bennet sisters in the marriage market
- **Darcy remains silent with her for half an hour** in Chapter 5, strengthening Mrs. Bennet's dislike of him

### Relationships among the Officers
- **Wickham–Denny:** close; they arrive together from London
- **Wickham–Carter:** fellow officers
- **Wickham–Colonel Forster:** subordinate and commander. In Chapter 46 Colonel Forster discloses Wickham's intention to flee, showing that **Wickham failed to gain Forster's trust**
- **Wickham is popular within the regiment:** “captivating,” despite gambling debts in Chapter 50

## 4. Collins's Patronage Network

### Lady Catherine's Web of Influence
```
Lady Catherine
├── Mr. Collins (clergyman at Hunsford; absolutely dependent)
│   └── Charlotte (indirectly dependent through Collins)
├── Mrs. Jenkinson (Anne de Bourgh's companion; employed)
├── Mrs. Younge (former governess; possibly recommended by Darcy)
├── Miss Pope (placed as a governess)
└── Lady Metcalfe and others (social patronage)

[Darcy is her nephew but is not subordinate to her]
```

### Collins's Social Position
- Loyal in both directions: to the **upper rank, Lady Catherine**, and to the **lower rank, his parishioners**
- Ambivalent toward the Bennets: they are relatives, but their property is his future estate
- After marrying Charlotte, he also attempts to cultivate the Lucas family, including Sir William

## 7. The Bingley Sisters' Social Ambitions (Inferred)

### Caroline in the Marriage Market
- **Darcy is her first choice**, one she cannot relinquish
- She considers anyone else only reluctantly
- She may panic as she enters her thirties
- At the end, Chapter 61, she pays Elizabeth “arrears of civility” to **preserve access to Darcy's cousins and other connections**

### Louisa Hurst's Marriage
- Marriage to Mr. Hurst represents “fashion rather than fortune,” Chapter 4
- **Effectively a failed marriage**—her husband cares only for food and cards
- She is Caroline's ally and consolation

## 8. The Provincial Marriage Market (Inferred)

### Unmarried Women in Meryton, apart from the Bennet Sisters
- **The Lucas sisters, four or five**, including Maria and Charlotte
- **Mrs. Long's two nieces**
- **The Miss Gouldings**, residents of Haye Park mentioned by Mrs. Bennet in Chapter 50
- **The Miss Robinsons**
- **The Miss Webbs**, mentioned in Chapter 29 and accomplished at the pianoforte
- **Miss Watson**, visited by Captain Carter in Chapter 7

### Unmarried Men
- **Dozens of officers**
- **Mr. Robinson**, Chapter 5
- **Mr. Goulding**, a neighbour

### Matrix of Marriage Prospects
- Bingley: every mother's target
- Darcy: too elevated and too proud, so generally avoided
- Wickham: charming but poor; viable only with an heiress such as Miss King
- Collins: secure but ridiculous; viable only for a pragmatist such as Charlotte

## 10. Tracing Wickham and Lydia's Flight (Reconstruction)

### Sequence Reconstructed from Clues in the Novel

```
[Sunday midnight] Depart Brighton by stagecoach
     ↓
[Early Monday] Arrive at Epsom; change stagecoaches
     ↓
[Monday morning] Arrive at Clapham → **abandon the stagecoach and take a hackney coach**
     ↓
[Monday morning] Enter London
     ↓
[Movements unknown] Wickham first goes to Mrs. Younge, per Mrs. Gardiner's Chapter 52 letter
     ↓
[Mrs. Younge refuses them] Move to another lodging in ---- Street
     ↓
[Several days later] Darcy bribes Mrs. Younge and learns their location
     ↓
[After two or three days of negotiation] Agreement to marry
     ↓
[Early August] Wedding at the Gardiners' house, attended by Darcy
```

### Wickham's True Intention (Inferred)
- **Absolutely no intention of marriage**, according to Denny's testimony in Chapter 46
- Plans to keep Lydia as a mistress temporarily and then abandon her
- Aims to clear his debts and begin again
- **Would not have married without Darcy's money**

## 11. Events Unseen in the Novel (Circumstantial Inference)

### Darcy–Bingley Negotiations at Pemberley, between Chapters 42 and 43
```
[Inferred Sequence]
- Bingley accompanies Darcy to Derbyshire, perhaps to hunt or simply visit
- Darcy arrives at Pemberley early in order to prepare to receive Bingley's party
- Bingley does not know Darcy's feelings
```

### Darcy's Secret Agreement with Uncle Gardiner (EVT-039-005)
```
- Saturday visit to the Gardiners' house
- Darcy: “I bore every expense—please make it appear that Mr. Gardiner did so.”
- Mr. Gardiner initially refuses out of pride
- Darcy argues: “The affair began with my own fault.”
- Agreement: Gardiner will outwardly receive the credit
```

### Darcy's Confession to Bingley about Separating the Couple (EVT-042-001)
```
- At Pemberley, Darcy confesses to Bingley
- “I misjudged whether Jane loved you.”
- “I concealed from you that Jane was staying in London.”
- Bingley's response: anger → forgiveness, consistent with his temperament
- Immediate decision to return to Netherfield
```

### Mr. Bennet's London Search, after EVT-038
```
- Travels with Colonel Forster
- May hire the Bow Street Runners, London's investigators
- No result; delegates the search to Gardiner and returns home
- His pride suffers severely, and he withdraws further afterward
```

## 12. Unseen Changes in Relationships after the Ending (Chapter 61)

### Daily Life at Pemberley (Inferred)
- Elizabeth becomes mistress of the house and works with Mrs. Reynolds
- Georgiana follows Elizabeth with a sisterly devotion
- Bingley and Jane move within thirty miles and visit often

### Wickham and Lydia's Chronic Dependence
- Ask their elder sisters for money every year
- Presumably **Elizabeth secretly assists them from her pin money**
- Darcy pretends not to know, or knows and tolerates it

### Kitty's Change
- Frequently visits Pemberley and Netherfield
- Becomes “much improved,” acquiring better social manners
- New prospects for marriage open

### Mary's Change
- Becomes her mother's only companion after her sisters leave home
- May join the social life of the sister who becomes Mrs. Bingley
- Her insecurity about her appearance diminishes

### Mr. and Mrs. Bennet
- Two daughters marry rich men; perhaps Mrs. Bennet's nerves settle
- Mr. Bennet remains withdrawn
- Mary alone remains at home

### Lady Catherine's Reconciliation
- Succumbs to curiosity
- Visits Pemberley
- Is compelled to observe formal civility toward Elizabeth
- No genuine friendship develops

### Miss Bingley's Hypocritical Friendship
- Offers “conciliatory civility” in order to gain access to Pemberley
- Her last card in the marriage market

### Darcy's Maternal Fitzwilliam Family
- **His uncle the earl** and **his aunt the countess**

- Uncle Gardiner's four children, who do appear
- Mr. and Mrs. Philips, presumed childless

### The Bingley Family's Relations
- None stated
