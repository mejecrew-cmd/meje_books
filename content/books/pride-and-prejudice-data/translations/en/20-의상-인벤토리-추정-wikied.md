# *Pride and Prejudice* — Costume Inventories (Estimated/Creative)

> ⚠️ **This document consists of estimates and creative reconstruction.** It reconstructs each character’s wardrobe for costume designers by combining fragments of dress explicitly mentioned in the novel—red coats, shoe-roses, a parasol, muddy petticoats—with Regency-era clothing standards and each character’s means and personality.

---

## Basics of Regency Women’s Dress (1811–12)

### By Type
- **Morning Dress:** Simple indoor dress with long sleeves, suitable for receiving callers
- **Walking Dress:** Outdoor wear, combining a bonnet and pelisse
- **Day Dress / Carriage Dress:** Formal wear for going out by carriage
- **Evening Dress:** Worn at dinner; short sleeves and a low décolletage
- **Ball Gown:** The most elaborate dress, often white
- **Riding Habit:** Modeled on menswear; worn with a hat and whip

### Fabrics
- **Muslin:** Summer fabric, often white
- **Silk:** Evening dresses and ball gowns
- **Wool / Bombazine:** Winter outerwear
- **Cambric:** Fine linen used for chemises
- **Calico:** Everyday cotton fabric
- **Lace:** Decoration

## 14. Changes in Relationships Expressed Through Dress

### Changes in Darcy’s Clothing
- Ch.3 ball: intimidating formal dress
- Ch.43 Pemberley: a more relaxed impression at home
- Ch.58 second proposal: possibly brighter colors

### Changes in Elizabeth’s Clothing
- Ch.7 muddy petticoat → symbol of freedom
- After marriage, as mistress of Pemberley → more formal clothes

### Lydia’s Clothing
- No change; the same impulsiveness before and after marriage
