# Colonel Fitzwilliam

## Basic Information
- **Original name:** Colonel Fitzwilliam
- **Korean rendering:** Colonel Fitzwilliam
- **Age:** about 30, estimated
- **Family:** second son of an earl; Darcy's cousin and Lady Catherine's nephew
- **Position:** army colonel
- **Residence:** London, through military service
- **First appearance:** chunk_19, Chap. 30

## Character Introduction
Darcy's cousin and Georgiana's co-guardian. Born the second son of an earl, he is a cheerful gentleman who has pursued a military career. Unlike Darcy, he is sociable and enjoys conversation, quickly becoming friendly with Elizabeth. He is not handsome, but wins favor through his manners and conversational ability. As a younger son excluded from inheriting the family estate, however, he faces the practical necessity of finding a wealthy wife. He is attracted to Elizabeth but acknowledges that his lack of fortune prevents him from courting her.

Narratively, Colonel Fitzwilliam is a decisive bearer of information. While staying at Rosings, he casually mentions that Darcy saved Bingley from an “imprudent marriage.” This ignites Elizabeth's anger and directly contributes to her rejection of Darcy's first proposal.

## Development by Event

### Rosings Episode, Ch. 30–33
- **EVT-026:** visits Rosings with Darcy at Easter. He converses cheerfully with Elizabeth and proves much more sociable than Darcy, visiting Hunsford Parsonage several times to see her.
- **EVT-027:** during a walk, he casually tells Elizabeth that Darcy recently saved a friend from an imprudent marriage. Elizabeth immediately realizes that he means the separation of Bingley and Jane. **This becomes one of her three reasons for rejecting Darcy.**

## Core Relationships
- **Darcy:** cousin and close relative; cooperates with Darcy as Georgiana's co-guardian
- **Elizabeth:** attracted to her but unable to court her because he lacks fortune; his careless remark triggers a narrative turning point
- **Lady Catherine:** maternal aunt; he visits out of social duty but does not flatter her excessively
- **Georgiana:** protects her as co-guardian

## Translation Notes
- Translate “Colonel” with the established term for the rank, a position of considerable standing in the period's military hierarchy.
- The socioeconomic constraints on a younger son derive from British primogeniture and may require a translator's note.
- Colonel Fitzwilliam's speech is lighter and more cheerful than Darcy's but not as simple as Bingley's. Use an intelligent yet pleasant middle register.
- “Fitzwilliam” is also Darcy's given name, Fitzwilliam Darcy, so avoid contextual confusion.
