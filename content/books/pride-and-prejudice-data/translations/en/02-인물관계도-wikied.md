# *Pride and Prejudice* — Character Relationship Map

## 1. Family Structures

### Bennet Family — Longbourn
```
Mr Bennet ─── Mrs Bennet (née Gardiner)
    │
    ├── Jane Bennet (eldest)        ──→ marries Mr Bingley
    ├── Elizabeth Bennet (second)   ──→ marries Mr Darcy
    ├── Mary Bennet (third)
    ├── Kitty/Catherine Bennet (fourth)
    └── Lydia Bennet (youngest)     ──→ marries Mr Wickham
```

### Mrs Bennet's Relatives
```
Mrs Bennet's younger brother ─── Mr Gardiner (London merchant)
                                  │
                                  └── Mrs Gardiner (connected to Derbyshire)
                                       + four children (two daughters, two sons)

Mrs Bennet's younger sister ─── Mrs Philips (lives in Meryton)
                                │
                                └── Mr Philips (attorney)
```

### Darcy Family — Pemberley
```
The late Mr Darcy ─── Lady Anne Darcy
    │                    │
    │                    └── sister: Lady Catherine de Bourgh
    │
    ├── Fitzwilliam Darcy          ──→ marries Elizabeth
    └── Georgiana Darcy (age 16)
         [joint guardians: Darcy + Colonel Fitzwilliam]
```

### de Bourgh Family — Rosings
```
The late Sir Lewis de Bourgh ─── Lady Catherine de Bourgh
                                  │
                                  └── Miss Anne de Bourgh
                                       + Mrs Jenkinson (companion/governess)
```

### Bingley Family — Netherfield
```
The late Mr Bingley (father, northern merchant)
    │
    ├── Charles Bingley       ──→ marries Jane
    ├── Caroline Bingley
    └── Louisa Bingley ─── Mr Hurst
         (Mrs Hurst)
```

### Lucas Family — Lucas Lodge
```
Sir William Lucas ─── Lady Lucas
    │
    ├── Charlotte Lucas (age 27) ──→ marries Mr Collins
    ├── Maria Lucas
    └── younger sons
```

### Collins
```
Mr Collins (clergyman and distant cousin of Mr Bennet)
    │
    └── legal heir to the Longbourn estate under the entail
         rector of Hunsford parish
         receives Lady Catherine's patronage
```

## 2. Evolution of the Core Relationships (Event Order)

### Darcy ↔ Elizabeth (the Novel's Central Axis)
```
EVT-003  [dislike begins] Darcy's “tolerable” insult → Elizabeth's dislike
EVT-004  [one-sided attraction] Darcy is drawn to her “fine eyes” (Elizabeth does not know)
EVT-009  [bewitched] Darcy is fully captivated → deliberately distances himself
EVT-016  [confrontation] dance at the ball + direct clash over Wickham
EVT-028  [rupture] first proposal/rejection: “last man in the world”
EVT-029  [turning point] Darcy's letter → truth revealed
EVT-030  [self-recognition] “Till this moment, I never knew myself”
EVT-035  [reassessment] Elizabeth rediscovers Darcy's character at Pemberley
EVT-036  [reunion] Darcy's completely changed manner
EVT-039  [recognition of love] “all love must be vain”—love named for the first time
EVT-042  [gratitude] Darcy's secret intervention revealed
EVT-046  [union] second proposal accepted
EVT-048  [marriage] mistress of Pemberley
```

### Bingley ↔ Jane
```
EVT-003  [first meeting] dance twice at the ball → mutual attraction
EVT-006  [deepening] sincere concern during Jane's illness
EVT-010  [parting] Jane returns home; Bingley regrets her leaving
EVT-019  [separation] Darcy and Miss Bingley conspire to withdraw to London
EVT-021  [despair] Miss Bingley's letter extinguishes Jane's hope
EVT-023  [hypocrisy exposed] Miss Bingley's coldness in London → friendship ends
EVT-037  [reunion] at Pemberley, Bingley says he has not seen Jane “since the 26th of November”
EVT-043  [return] Darcy reverses the separation → return to Netherfield
EVT-044  [engagement] renewed proposal + acceptance
EVT-048  [marriage]
```

### Wickham ↔ Multiple Characters
```
Wickham ↔ Darcy:
  - childhood: raised together on the same estate (godson of the late Mr Darcy)
  - voluntarily gives up a £3,000 living → dissipation → demands it again → refused
  - attempts to seduce Georgiana (revealed in EVT-029)
  - final confrontation after Lydia's flight (EVT-038–040)

Wickham ↔ Elizabeth:
  EVT-014  gains her trust through false testimony
  EVT-022  turns to Miss King → Elizabeth's illusion begins to dissolve
  EVT-030  letter reveals his true nature → trust collapses completely
  EVT-033  final meeting—Elizabeth detects his hypocrisy directly

Wickham ↔ Lydia:
  EVT-038  flee from Brighton (with no intention of marrying)
  EVT-040  Darcy's secret intervention brings about the marriage
```

### Collins ↔ the Bennets ↔ Charlotte
```
EVT-011  Collins visits Longbourn (the personification of the entail)
EVT-012  intended bride: Jane → Elizabeth
EVT-017  Elizabeth rejects his proposal
EVT-018  Mr Bennet takes Elizabeth's side
EVT-020  Charlotte accepts Collins → rift in her friendship with Elizabeth
```

## 3. Class and Economic Relationship Map

### Ranking by Wealth
```
[highest]
  Mr Darcy           £10,000 a year + Pemberley estate + London townhouse
  Lady Catherine     Rosings estate + immense fortune

[upper]
  Mr Bingley         approximately £100,000 inherited (£4,000–5,000 a year) / no estate purchased
  Miss de Bourgh     heiress to Rosings (amount unknown, immense)
  Georgiana Darcy    £30,000 dowry

[middle]
  Mr Bennet          £2,000 a year (the entail disadvantages his daughters)
  Mr Collins         income from Hunsford parish (quite secure)

[lower]
  Mr Wickham         penniless + in debt (rescued by Darcy)
  each Bennet sister approximately £200 after their mother's death (4% government stock)
```

### Patronage Relationships
```
Lady Catherine ──[right of presentation to a living]──→ Mr Collins
Mr Darcy ──[rights of presentation in several parishes]──→ various clergymen
Mr Darcy ──[purchases an ensign's commission]──→ Mr Wickham (secret intervention)
```

### Entail Chain
```
Mr Bennet's estate (Longbourn)
    │
    └──[entail]──→ Mr Collins (male heir)
                    │
                    └──→ Charlotte Lucas Collins (future mistress)

※ Mrs Bennet and her five daughters are excluded from the inheritance
```

## 5. Classification by Emotional/Relational Type

### Love and Marriage
| Relationship | Nature | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Darcy ↔ Elizabeth | genuine love overcoming pride and prejudice | marriage |
| Bingley ↔ Jane | pure mutual affection tested by outside intervention | marriage |
| Wickham ↔ Lydia | impulse and imprudence, secured with money | marriage (unhappy) |
| Collins ↔ Charlotte | practical compromise, an unemotional contract | marriage (stable but arid) |
| Mr Bennet ↔ Mrs Bennet | a youthful mistake, long since extinguished | marriage (cynical coexistence) |

### Hostility and Rivalry
| Relationship | Nature |
|---|---|
| Miss Bingley → Elizabeth | one-sided jealousy and hostility over Darcy |
| Miss Bingley → Jane | superficial friendship + covert separation |
| Lady Catherine → Elizabeth | class contempt + defense of the planned marriage to Miss de Bourgh |
| Darcy ↔ Wickham | childhood companions turned enemies (the Georgiana affair) |

### Friendship and Trust
| Relationship | Nature |
|---|---|
| Elizabeth ↔ Jane | the deepest sisterly bond; confidantes |
| Elizabeth ↔ Charlotte | fractured over conflicting views of marriage, then partially restored |
| Elizabeth ↔ Mrs Gardiner | wise adviser and emotional supporter |
| Darcy ↔ Bingley | opposite temperaments, but closest friends |
| Darcy ↔ Colonel Fitzwilliam | cousins and Georgiana's joint guardians |

### Patronage and Dependence
| Relationship | Nature |
|---|---|
| Lady Catherine → Collins | absolute patronage and flattery founded on the right of presentation |
| Darcy → Wickham | his father's legacy → betrayal → secret rescue |
| Darcy → Bingley | friendship in which Darcy claims superior judgment (separation/reversal) |

## 6. Core Timeline of Relationship Changes for Translators

Forms of address, tone, and attitude between characters change with events. Consult the timeline below when translating:

| Point | Darcy → Elizabeth | Elizabeth → Darcy | Bingley → Jane | Wickham → Elizabeth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Chapters 1–6 | indifference → concealed attraction | simple dislike | attraction begins | not yet appeared |
| Chapters 7–13 | bewitched + restraint | dislike maintained | sincere concern | not yet appeared |
| Chapters 14–17 | distancing | prejudice entrenched by Wickham's testimony | attraction peaks | charming victim |
| Chapters 18–23 | painful attraction | hostility maximized | separated | turns to Miss King |
| Chapters 24–34 | restraint fails → proposal | “last man in the world” | separated + despairing | relationship disappears |
| Chapters 35–40 | self-defense through the letter | recognizes prejudice + shame | no change | disillusionment complete |
| Chapters 41–45 | completely changed manner | respect + gratitude + unnamed attraction | longing maintained | contempt for Wickham |
| Chapters 46–54 | secret rescue | recognizes love + “all vain” | return + renewed approach | fugitive/fraud |
| Chapters 55–61 | proposes again | accepts | engagement + marriage | family disgrace |
