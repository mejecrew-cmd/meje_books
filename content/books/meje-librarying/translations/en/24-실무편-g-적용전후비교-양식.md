# Part 6. Practice G. Before-and-After Comparison Template

This practice template records the utility of librarying without exaggeration. The most dangerous part of a white paper is making results look impressive by mixing measured values with estimates. Therefore, label every figure as `measured / estimated / illustrative example`.

## Measurement principles

First, **state the conditions together.** Time is meaningful only when the record also says whether the basis is 1,500 headwords, how many input documents there were, how many people worked, and how many AI collaborators ran in parallel.

Second, **keep the before-and-after baseline identical.** A comparison is meaningless when the scopes of the existing method and the librarying method differ. For example, “rough setting confirmation” in the old method and a “validated Vault” in the librarying method are not the same task.

Third, **include quality metrics.** Reducing time alone is insufficient. Include metrics that show operational quality, such as omission rate, link errors, inconsistent translation notation, and onboarding time for new workers.

## Illustrative example

The table below is an illustrative example for understanding the format, based on **“Land of Nubi.”** When including it in a published white paper, replace it with measured values or clearly retain the illustrative-example label.

| Metric | Existing method | Librarying method | Type |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Organizing 1,500 headwords for “Land of Nubi” | about 3–6 months | about 9–16 hours | Illustrative example; based on the main-text time budget |
| Canonical-keyword duplicates | Not measurable | Target: 0 | Methodology standard |
| Broken links | Not measurable | Target: 0 | Validation standard |
| Grasping the core of “Land of Nubi” for a new worker | 1–3 months | Prioritize learning 100–150 hubs | Illustrative example; requires project-specific measurement |

## Interpretation standard

Librarying efficiency does not simply mean “faster.” More precisely, it reduces verification costs and error-recovery costs within a repeatable procedure. Therefore, a before-and-after comparison should look beyond time saved and include the cost of handling the same IP again next month and next year.
