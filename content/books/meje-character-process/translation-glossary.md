## Locale and style rules

- Preserve heading hierarchy, paragraph order, lists, blockquotes, code fences, examples, numeric values, and copyright lines.
- Translate every explanatory parenthesis; do not summarize or silently omit examples.
- Use direct instructional prose, without marketing embellishment or stronger scientific claims.
- Preserve established titles and personal names in each target language where available.
- English uses publication prose and gender-neutral singular *they* when the Korean source does not specify gender.
- Japanese uses instructional です・ます prose while examples may retain literary narration.
- Traditional Chinese uses Taiwan-oriented terms such as `卡司`, `身分`, `使用者`, and `工作階段` where natural.
- Simplified Chinese requires a separate Mainland lexical review after script conversion; use terms such as `角色阵容`, `身份`, `用户`, and `会话` where natural.
- A Traditional-to-Simplified character conversion alone is not sufficient for `translated` status. Confirm Mainland terminology and sentence flow separately.
- Neurodiversity, disability, sexuality, gender identity, race, and trauma must retain the source's non-stigmatizing distinctions.
