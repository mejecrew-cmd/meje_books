# <Seoyeongak> — Before a Book Enters Translation

An apple is not Apple. A pear does not taste as good as *pear*.

Every day, our content is carried into 169 countries and 39 languages. When a small misalignment accumulates 169 times, a character's face hardens into something different. That is why MEJE Works Corp. created a process for weighing every word before a work moves into another language. Its name is **<Seoyeongak>**.

This thirteen-part series records the entire Seoyeongak process as it is applied to Jane Austen's ***Pride and Prejudice***. We will follow it to the end: how a work's time is broken apart, how the changes of thirty-four characters are traced, and how thirty-five kinds of materials are built around a single book—the household we set between ourselves before a book enters translation.

Once a week, for thirteen weeks.
