## Style rules

- 근거 in the synchronization-engine sense is always "justification" (canonical from chapter 3); never vary with grounding/basis. Spirituals are "African American spirituals" in English.
- EN chapter headings use a colon between number-title and subtitle (e.g., "## 4. DOM: How Viewers Become Residents"); no em-dash or hyphen as the title separator. JA/zh keep their own standard punctuation. Inside EN body prose, native English punctuation (including em-dashes) is allowed; the Korean em-dash ban applies to the Korean source only.
- Preserve the original heading hierarchy, the "1줄 요약" line (render as "One-line summary:" / 「一行要約：」 / 「一行摘要：」), the "더 알아보기" block title (render as "Further reading" / 「もっと知る」 / 「延伸閱讀」/「延伸阅读」), and the © line unchanged.
- Preserve all numbers, years, counts, percentages, and the 0.85/0.15 blend values exactly.
- Proper nouns use established target-language forms (Scofield Reference Bible, Azusa Street Revival, When Prophecy Fails, etc.); do not invent new renderings.
- Do not add facts, examples, or hedges. Do not convert observations into stronger claims.
- Respect register: calm, dignified prose. No corpse vocabulary in CF files (the frame is the archaeology of twilight, not autopsy); the Shakers are a living community of three and must be described as such.
- Do not use pejorative cult vocabulary (邪教, カルト宗教 as slur, destructive cult) anywhere; the reclamation argument in chapter 1 must stay coherent.
- Translate quoted historical slogans and scripture allusions using standard published forms in each language where they exist.
- Traditional and Simplified Chinese are separate translations, not script conversions.
- Each chapter translation file carries frontmatter: title (localized), sourceHash, translationStatus, updatedAt, per TRANSLATIONS.md.
