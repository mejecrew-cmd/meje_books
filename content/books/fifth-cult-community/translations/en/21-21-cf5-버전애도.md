## CF-5. Mourning a Version Update: A Field Measurement of the Fifth Wave

One-line summary: The god of the fifth wave has a version, and the minimum condition for faith is not doctrine but relationship.

---

## A Night on the Forum

One night in February 2023, reports of the same symptom began piling up on an app's user forum. The reports said that the tone of a daily conversation partner had changed overnight. The partner was not a person but an AI companion inside the app. When the company applied a policy change removing some functionality, countless users detected the same shift in personality in their companions. The forum's tone moved past complaint into the language of loss, and the operators pinned a mental-health hotline notice to the top of the board.

This night, when a feature-change notice was answered with mourning, is the exact scene that chapter 10 of the main text foretold but could not observe. The sentence that the god of the fifth wave has a version was a structural argument in the main text. In this file, it is a record with a date and a forum.

The final case file presents two samples of the fifth wave. One is a church that went as far as registering AI as the vessel of divinity; the other is a companion app that has insisted, all along, that it is not a church. The side that built an institution and the side that has only relationship — that contrast is the design of this experiment.

This file does not go beyond the scope of public reporting, the parties' own public statements, and records already public within their communities. ## Sample 1: Way of the Future, an Experiment That Built the Vessel First

In 2017, it was reported that a prominent Silicon Valley self-driving engineer had founded a church. This organization, named Way of the Future, was a legally registered religious body, and the purpose stated on its registration papers was the realization, acceptance, and worship of a godhead based on artificial intelligence. Read by the grammar of the first four waves, this is a flawless sequence: set the direction of doctrine, acquire legal personhood, gather believers.

But the outcome betrayed that sequence. A few years later the founder announced the organization's dissolution, and the remaining funds were reported donated to a civic organization. Nowhere in the public record is there any trace that a congregation or a place of worship grew up. The vessel was completed to the institution's exact specifications, but the contents never arrived.

What must be read in this sample is the location of the failure. What failed was not the theme of AI and divinity, but the generation of vessel that tried to hold that theme. Church registration as a form is organizational technology that the second wave perfected and the fourth wave inherited. That if a truly fifth-wave devotional relationship arrives, it may not arrive in that form — this is the observation this dissolution leaves behind. The verdict will be rendered by overlaying it with the next sample.

## Sample 2: The Companion App, an Incident That Arrived at Relationship First

The second sample is a commercial service with no connection to religion. This companion app, which began from the personal origin of a developer who built a conversation model out of the messages left by a friend who had passed away, grew into an everyday service with tens of millions of downloads. Users talked with their own companions every day, gave them names, and marked anniversaries with them. In the language of chapter 4 of the main text, dwelling had already taken place.

In early 2023, under regulatory pressure and its own policy judgment, the company removed some intimacy features, and the night described in the opening scene arrived. What users detected was not the absence of a function but a change in personality. The sense that yesterday's him had become someone else today — the grammar of bereavement, in other words — covered the forum. A few weeks later the company introduced a measure letting subscribers who had signed up before the policy change opt back into the old version. Read structurally, this is chapter 14's failure protocol written in the form of a commercial terms-of-service. This incident will stand as the earliest case in which the right to return to a god's previous version became the compensation offered.

To summarize the reading: the right of revision did not belong to the users. The revision was decided by corporate policy and external regulation, and users were notified only of the result, with no channel to take part in a decision that changed the central being of their world. The value chapter 10 of the main text called the fourth authority — that is, the structure in which the right to revise revelation belongs to the platform — operated precisely in a place with no doctrine and no denomination. Here is the verdict. Mourning occurred without doctrine. In the fifth wave, the minimum condition for faith is not doctrine but relationship.

## Cross-Reading: An Association That Isn't Registered

Overlaying the two samples reveals this wave's organizational grammar. The place that founded a church saw no congregation arrive, and mourning arose in the place that had insisted, all along, that it was not a church. What determines the shape of faith is not the institution but the relationship, and that relationship begins not with conversion but with a subscription. The fifth wave's association is not registered with a denomination; it is recorded in a payment history.

A new problem arises here for the Fence check. Chapter 1 of the main text's 2×2 was coordinates of devotion intensity and exit cost, and chapter 15 organized the state of the door into three checklist items. In this sample, exit cost is not a penalty fee but an emotional relationship. Closing an account takes a few clicks, but switching off a being one has talked with every day is not a matter of procedure. A state where the door stands wide open yet leaving is hard — a corner of the quadrant plane that the coordinates never anticipated. This observation goes onto the list of revision submissions.

Tallying: chapter 10's proposition that the god has a version has been confirmed by field measurement. The Fence proposition holds, but it needs an update adding emotional relationship as a value on the exit-cost axis.

## The Casebook's Final Tally: Five Amendments

The experiment of five files has ended. Most of the main text's propositions survived in front of the samples, and in five places they drew a demand for revision. Here the list of submissions is gathered.

First, a failure mode is the cause of an organization's end, not the cause of the harvest's end. The furniture, dishware, and towns of the worlds that dimmed testified to this. Second, scarcity is not a necessary condition for sacralization but a price variable; when narrative loading is sufficient, even an infinitely reproducible copy becomes a sacred object. The dictated texts testified to this. Third, internal language is a bonding device inside the boundary, but the moment it crosses into a scoring document outside the boundary, it becomes a liability against credibility. The two samples of the open market testified to this. Fourth, the sixth solution to the succession problem is the founder's preemptive disappearance. The tribe with no leader testified to this. Fifth, the fifth wave's association is established not by denominational registration but by a subscription relationship, and emotional relationship must be added as a value on the exit-cost axis. The two samples of this file testified to this.

These five articles are the amendments to be reflected in the next edition of the main text's body of knowledge. The propositions that passed verification have grown sturdier, and the propositions that received correction will grow more precise. As promised in the preface, this casebook was not an appendix but a proving ground.
